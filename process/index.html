Our process

To understand how technology can meet our vision and serve the California public, we held more than 60 interviews and facilitated sessions with:

  • technology executives and leaders (Agency Chief Information Officers, Chief Information Officers, and the California Department of Technology’s leadership and enterprise architects)
  • agency, department and business executives and leaders
  • staff representing the legislative branch, judicial branch
  • local government leaders; and
  • vendor and civic tech community.

Our research involved surveying nearly 8,000 state staff across 19 departments, supervisors and managers at the California Department of Technology, chief information officers. We surveyed the technology vendor community and ran an open collaboration session, reaching out to companies who have never worked with the state.

We reviewed technology reports from the Legislative Analyst’s Office and the California State Auditor, and the Employment Development Department Strike Team’s detailed assessment and recommendations.

Further afield, we reviewed industry reports and researched the experiences, successes and challenges of civic technologists and governments across America and the world.
